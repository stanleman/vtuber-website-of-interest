Definition

Vtubers (ブイチューバー)

A VTuber is an online entertainer who uses a virtual avatar generated using computer graphics. Real-time motion capture software or technology are often—but not always—used to capture movement. The digital trend originated in Japan in the mid-2010s, and has become an international online phenomenon in the 2020s.A majority of VTubers are English and Japanese-speaking YouTubers or live streamers who use avatar designs. By 2020, there were more than 10,000 active VTubers. Although the term is an allusion to the video platform YouTube, they also use websites such as Niconico, Twitch, Facebook, Twitter, and Bilibili.

Timeline

A documented timeline of the significant events throughout vtuber history.

  1. June 13 2011 UK-based Japanese vlogger Ami Yamato uploaded her first video, which featured an animated, virtual avatar speaking to the camera.
  2. 7 July 2015 The FaceRig indie software was released, becoming the first software suite that enabled live avatars at home via face motion capture.
  3. 9 December 2015 The Live2D software module in FaceRig enabling 2D avatars was added soon after in collaboration with Live2D, Inc.
  4. 29 November 2016 Kizuna AI, created by Activ8 was the first vtuber to acheieve breakout popularity. She was the first to coin and use the term "virtual YouTuber".
  5. 7 September 2017 Cover Corp debuted Tokino Sora, the first VTuber using the company's avatar capture software.
  6. February 2018 Anycolor Inc. founded the vtuber agency, Nijisanji, which helped popularise the use of Live2D models instead of the prior focus on 3D models.
  7. May - Sep 2018 The first generation of Hololive VTubers debuted from May to June 2018, and a second generation followed in August and September.
  8. 2018 - 2020 Agencies like Hololive and Nijisanji created branches in China, South Korea, Indonesia, and India, as well as English-language branches targeting a global audience.
  9. 2020 The COVID-19 pandemic led to an overall increase in viewer ship of video game live streaming in general in 2020, which contributed to the growth of VTubers.
  10. August 2020 Seven of the ten largest Super Chat earners of all time on YouTube were VTubers, including Hololive member Kiryu Coco at number one.
  11. 2021 - present The vtuber industry is still steadily growing, with approximately 50,000 vtubers streaming actively as of 2024.

Process

Illustration

Tools used:  

 Clip Studio Paint, 

 Photoshop, 

 Krita, 

 Procreate

All vtuber models are first sketched into life on the platforms listed above. Color, shading, lineart and other details are then added. Each part of the model drawing and seperated are labelled before exporting into a rigging software.

The reason for seperation is to ease the process of rigging the movement of each individual part later down the line.

Rigging

Tools used:  

 Live2D Cubism, 

 Inochi2D 

Rigging is the process of animating a finished vtuber model to give it movement, different poses and facial expressions.

Face Tracking

Tools used:  

 VTube Studio, 

 PrprLive, 

 FaceRig 

After illustration and rigging, everything is put through a face tracking software so it can be used in real-time by vtubers.

Agencies

Agencies are companies that have invested in Virtual YouTubers as a form of entertainment. Within the vtubing industry, there exists the "Big 3" of agencies, namely Hololive, Vshojo & Nijisanji. These 3 agencies paved the way for vtubers and are currently leading the development of the industry.

There also exists vtubers who operate independent of an agency, called indies.
